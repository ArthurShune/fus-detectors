\begin{abstract}
Functional ultrasound (fUS) and ultrafast Doppler imaging face severe interference from tissue motion, phase aberrations, and aliased high-velocity flow, which can obscure slow microvascular dynamics. Low-rank clutter suppression methods such as motion-compensated singular value decomposition (MC--SVD) are widely used, but can degrade in extreme clutter and in the far right tail of flow evidence.

Motivated by airborne radar and RF array processing, we introduce a space--time adaptive processing (STAP) framework for fUS slow-time flow detection. The method operates on localized spatiotemporal tiles with a fixed-parameter operating profile (tile geometry, slow-time aperture, Doppler band geometry, and covariance conditioning) and produces a matched-subspace STAP score. An optional knowledge-aided (KA) spatial-spectral prior uses local band-energy features to apply a conservative, shrink-only penalty in high-risk regions while protecting a high-confidence set; by construction, the post-KA score is non-increasing on penalized pixels. We also study conditional execution as a decision-rule variant that skips STAP outside a flow-proxy region and quantify its leakage sensitivity.

Quantitative detection performance is evaluated using a strict low-FPR operating discipline (typically $10^{-4}$--$10^{-3}$, subject to empirical support), reflecting the sensitivity of downstream functional mapping to rare flow artifacts and consistent with tail-rate control philosophies in radar detection and tracking. We report results on labeled k-Wave brain simulations and on structurally labeled \emph{in vitro} phantom acquisitions (Gammex; labels derived from B-mode geometry), and we benchmark against MC--SVD, robust PCA, and tensor HOSVD baselines as well as conventional short-ensemble Doppler scores. We further report labeled within-ensemble motion ladders on phantom data via per-shot motion injection, and label-free stability under controlled motion corruption on kilohertz ultrasound localization microscopy (ULM) IQ blocks. Because most open in-vivo fUS releases do not preserve raw high-PRF complex IQ with independent $H_1/H_0$ labels, we restrict quantitative ROC claims to labeled simulations and structurally labeled phantoms, using in-vivo data only for robustness and telemetry audits. All experiments and figures are accompanied by a command-level reproducibility manifest in the Supplementary Material (exact scripts, arguments, and expected artifact paths).
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Microvascular flow detection in ultrasound is an extreme-tail problem: flow evidence occupies a small fraction of pixels while tissue motion, phase aberration, and aliased high-velocity clutter induce heavy-tailed background statistics. In this regime, improvements in global summaries such as AUC can be operationally uninformative; the key requirement is stable detection at ultra-low false-positive rates.

We address this setting with a localized, STAP-inspired detector in which adaptation is temporal (slow-time), while spatial localization enters through tile-local covariance estimation. Instead of relying only on global low-rank suppression, we whiten local slow-time snapshots and score energy in a flow subspace, targeting spatially varying interference patterns that dominate extreme-tail false alarms.

We further introduce a knowledge-aided (KA) shrink-only regularization layer driven by local band-energy features. KA can suppress high-risk tail events without score inflation: post-KA scores are pointwise non-increasing, and a protected set is explicitly preserved.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item A tile-based temporal STAP detector with matched-subspace interpretation, evaluated at strict low-FPR operating points.
  \item A feature-conditioned KA regularizer with shrink-only monotonicity and protected-set invariance.
  \item A leakage-controlled conditional-execution variant treated explicitly as a changed decision rule (not a cost-free speedup).
  \item Regime-stratified evidence across labeled simulations, structurally labeled Gammex flow phantom sequences, and label-free instrumentation datasets.
\end{itemize}

Claims are stratified by data regime and summarized in Table~\ref{tab:claims_discipline}. Quantitative detection efficacy is limited to regimes with independent $H_1/H_0$ labels; unlabeled in-vivo IQ and PD-only datasets are used for instrumentation and robustness analyses.

\section{Background and Problem Formulation}
\label{sec:background-problem}

\paragraph{Problem setup.}
For each spatial location (or tile-aggregated snapshot), let $x\in\mathbb{C}^{L_t}$ denote a slow-time vector. We consider
\[
H_0:\ x=c+n,\qquad H_1:\ x=s+c+n,
\]
where $s$ is flow-consistent signal, $c$ is structured clutter/motion, and $n$ is residual noise. In practice, $H_0$ is spatially nonstationary and heavy-tailed, motivating local covariance adaptation and tail-focused evaluation.

\paragraph{Core notation and bands.}
We use band operators $(\Po,\Pf,\Pg,\Pa)$ for clutter/DC, flow, guard, and alias regions, respectively. For short ensembles, these are exact orthogonal DFT-bin projectors; for longer apertures they are leakage-controlled analysis operators and are treated as band-energy statistics with explicit sanity sentinels (\SuppOrApp{app:alg-details}).

\subsection{Related context}
\textbf{STAP and knowledge-aided STAP.} Classical MVDR/LCMV/STAP and knowledge-aided variants shape covariances using external structure \cite{Capon1969Beamformer,FrostBeamformer, WardSTAPTutorial, GuerciSTAPBook2003,WicksKASTAP2006,MelvinGuerciKAParadigm2006}. Our spatial-spectral prior formalizes when such transforms are allowed to act in fUS and keeps them inert when data are out of regime.

\textbf{Ultrasound clutter suppression.} Spatiotemporal SVD clutter filters are a standard tool in ultrafast Doppler and fUS \cite{DemeneClutterSVD2015,BarangerAdaptiveSVD2018,Mace2013,Rabut2020,ICDFUS2021}. Robust PCA and tensor/HOSVD variants have also been studied for clutter suppression and motion robustness \cite{CandesRPCA,HuangAdaptiveHOSVD2024}. We benchmark STAP against MC--SVD, RPCA, and HOSVD baselines in brain-like regimes and report conventional short-ensemble Doppler comparators on phantom data.

\textbf{Relation to eigen-based and local adaptive clutter filtering.} Eigen-based clutter filter design for ultrasound color flow imaging and block-wise/local adaptive clutter filters are closely related ultrasound-native approaches to covariance/eigenspace adaptation \cite{YuLovstakkenEigenReview2010,SongBlockwiseLocal2017,CheeGPUEigen2017}. Our contribution differs in the \emph{detector} formulation and evaluation discipline: we whiten slow-time snapshots using tile-local conditioned covariances and score energy in an explicitly defined flow subspace (matched-subspace statistic), evaluate at empirically supported ultra-low FPR operating points, and treat compute gating (conditional execution) as a distinct decision rule with leakage controls.

\textbf{Twinkling artifact and Doppler phantoms.} The Doppler ``twinkling artifact'' has been documented as a persistent color-flow phenomenon on calcifications and other strong reflectors, and is widely used as a diagnostic benchmark for Doppler processing chains. Leonov \emph{et al.}\ provide an open-access database of raw beamformer signals with phantoms including a flow-through Gammex phantom and calculi/metal/plastic objects \cite{LeonovTwinklingDD2021}. We use these data to (i) obtain a structurally labeled phantom ROC via B-mode-only tube segmentation and (ii) construct an artifact-heavy KA hygiene regime where the prior penalty is expected to be conservative and auditable.

\textbf{fUS detection and hemodynamic analysis.} GLM-based detection and atlas-driven ROI analysis are common in hemodynamic fUS \cite{Demeneglmband2016,UrbanWholeBrain2015}; our hemodynamic STAP variant reuses the same banded temporal machinery on PD time series and adds an alias-aware shrink-only prior penalty as a safety layer.

\subsection{Baseline clutter suppression and score conventions}
\paragraph{Beamformed slow-time data.}
Let $X[t,y,x]\in\mathbb{C}$ denote a beamformed slow-time IQ movie over $t=0,\dots,T-1$ and spatial indices $(y,x)$. We first apply a baseline clutter-suppression operator (e.g.\ motion-compensated SVD), producing a residual cube $X_{\mathrm{base}}[t,y,x]$ used for both baseline scoring and as the input to STAP.

\paragraph{Power Doppler proxy and baseline scores.}
From the baseline-filtered cube we define a conventional power Doppler (PD) brightness proxy (used only for deterministic preprocessing such as proxy masks),
\[
  \mathrm{PD}^{\uparrow}(y,x)=\frac{1}{T}\sum_{t=0}^{T-1}\left|X_{\mathrm{base}}(t,y,x)\right|^2.
\]
For quantitative detection and thresholding we operate strictly on the right tail of score distributions and denote the baseline right-tail score as $S_{\mathrm{base}}(y,x)$ (power Doppler on the baseline-filtered cube). For short-ensemble regimes we additionally report auxiliary, conventional baseline comparators (e.g.\ log-power Doppler and Kasai lag-1 magnitude) to avoid baseline-score idiosyncrasies dominating conclusions (Notation).
\noindent Note: $\mathrm{PD}^{\uparrow}$ is the same underlying power statistic as $S_{\mathrm{base}}$ (up to fixed scaling); we keep a separate name to enforce the policy that proxy masks are used only for deterministic preprocessing and diagnostics, not as quantitative efficacy claims.

\section{Method}
\label{sec:method}
\label{sec:stap-architecture}
\label{sec:ka-contract-v2} % Backward-compatible label used throughout.

\paragraph{Algorithm 1 (KA--STAP pipeline, fixed profile).}
\begin{enumerate}[nosep,leftmargin=1.6em,label=\arabic*.]
  \item Baseline clutter suppression (e.g.\ MC--SVD) produces a residual cube $X_{\mathrm{base}}$ and baseline score $S_{\mathrm{base}}(y,x)$.
  \item For each tile $\Omega_i$: form slow-time snapshots $\{x_{i,k}\}_{k=1}^K$ (spatial sampling; optional Hankelization), estimate conditioned covariance $R_i$, and whiten $y_{i,k}=R_i^{-1/2}x_{i,k}$.
  \item Compute the matched-subspace statistic $S_{\mathrm{stap,pre}}(i)=\frac{1}{K}\sum_k y_{i,k}\Hh \Pf\, y_{i,k}$ and aggregate over overlapping tiles to obtain $S_{\mathrm{stap,pre}}(y,x)$.
  \item Compute local features $\phi_i=[m_{\mathrm{alias}}(i),\ r_g(i),\ c_f(i)]^\top$.
  \item If KA enabled: determine regime $\mathcal{R}_0/\mathcal{R}_1/\mathcal{R}_2$ and compute shrink-only weight $w(\phi_i)\in(0,1]$ (protected-set invariance); output $S_{\mathrm{stap,post}}(y,x)=w\,S_{\mathrm{stap,pre}}(y,x)$ and audit logs (region, reason, risk mode).
  \item Else: output $S_{\mathrm{stap,post}}(y,x)=S_{\mathrm{stap,pre}}(y,x)$.
\end{enumerate}
\noindent In practice STAP is intended as a second-stage residual covariance whitener: a coarse baseline clutter filter removes gross stationary tissue clutter, and STAP targets the remaining structured, spatially varying interference that dominates extreme-tail false alarms. A STAP-only ablation on phantom data is provided in \SuppOrApp{app:supp-results}.

\subsection{Temporal STAP core and Doppler band geometry}
\paragraph{Tile-based STAP.}
To mitigate the degraded tail sensitivity of global low-rank filters in high-clutter regimes, we apply a localized temporal STAP core on overlapping spatial tiles. We use ``STAP'' in a STAP-inspired sense: the adaptive filter operates in slow time, while spatial localization enters through tile-local covariance adaptation. Let $\Omega_i$ denote tile $i$ and $L_t$ the slow-time aperture. From $X_{\mathrm{base}}$ we construct a set of slow-time training snapshots $\{x_{i,k}\in\mathbb{C}^{L_t}\}_{k=1}^K$ for each tile (via spatial sampling and, when applicable, Hankelization in slow time). Here $K$ denotes the number of training snapshots per tile; in the fully Hankelized case $K=T-L_t+1$ per spatial sample (or a subsampled variant when training support is capped). We estimate a local covariance
\[
  \wh R_i := \frac{1}{K}\sum_{k=1}^K x_{i,k}x_{i,k}\Hh,
\]
apply conditioning/diagonal loading to obtain $R_i$, and whiten snapshots as $y_{i,k}:=R_i^{-1/2}x_{i,k}$.
Figure~\ref{fig:hankelization_local_stap} illustrates the local tile extraction and (optional) Hankelization used to form $\wh R_i$ and contrasts it with the global Casorati stacking used by MC--SVD.

\paragraph{Band operators and matched-subspace STAP score.}
We partition the Doppler spectrum using band projectors $(\Po,\Pf,\Pg,\Pa)$ (Notation). For short ensembles, these are exact, orthogonal DFT-bin projectors with $\Po+\Pf+\Pg+\Pa=I$; for longer apertures they are leakage-controlled operators that are approximately orthogonal and are treated as band-energy statistics. We define a matched-subspace STAP test statistic by flow-subspace energy on whitened snapshots, e.g.,
\[
  S_{\mathrm{stap,pre}}(i) := \frac{1}{K}\sum_{k=1}^K y_{i,k}\Hh \Pf\, y_{i,k},
\]
and form a pixel-level score map $S_{\mathrm{stap,pre}}(y,x)$ by overlap-add aggregation of the contributing tile statistics. This pre-KA score is the primary STAP detector output used for quantitative reporting unless explicitly stated otherwise.

\paragraph{Regularization and self-normalization avoidance.}
If we whitened using the exact sample covariance formed from the same snapshots being scored, the statistic collapses: the whitened sample covariance becomes (approximately) identity and averaged subspace energies concentrate near constants (e.g.\ $\tr(\Pf)$), destroying discrimination. We avoid this by whitening with a \emph{regularized} covariance $R_i$ (robust estimation plus shrinkage/diagonal loading), so that typically $R_i \neq \wh R_i$ and the matched-subspace energy remains informative. We do not rely on an analytic null distribution for CFAR operation; operating points are empirically calibrated on labeled $H_0$ sets under the fixed-profile protocol of Section~\ref{sec:eval}.

\paragraph{Spatial-spectral feature vector.}
In addition to the detector score, we compute per-tile band energies
\[
  \begin{aligned}
    E_f(i) &:= \frac{1}{K}\sum_{k=1}^K y_{i,k}\Hh \Pf\, y_{i,k},\\
    E_g(i) &:= \frac{1}{K}\sum_{k=1}^K y_{i,k}\Hh \Pg\, y_{i,k},\\
    E_a(i) &:= \frac{1}{K}\sum_{k=1}^K y_{i,k}\Hh \Pa\, y_{i,k},\\
    E_o(i) &:= \frac{1}{K}\sum_{k=1}^K y_{i,k}\Hh \Po\, y_{i,k},
  \end{aligned}
\]
and a total $E_{\mathrm{tot}}(i):=E_f(i)+E_g(i)+E_a(i)+E_o(i)+\epsilon$ with $\epsilon>0$. We use ratios of these energies as label-free statistics for interpretation and for the KA prior, e.g.,
\[
  m_{\mathrm{alias}}(i):=\log\frac{E_a(i)+\epsilon}{E_f(i)+\epsilon},\qquad
  m_{\mathrm{guard}}(i):=\log\frac{E_g(i)+\epsilon}{E_f(i)+\epsilon},\qquad
  r_g(i):=\frac{E_g(i)}{E_{\mathrm{tot}}(i)}.
\]
For short ensembles, we additionally compute a non-DC peak-frequency indicator from a pooled whitened tile vector $z_i:=\frac{1}{K}\sum_k y_{i,k}$ and its DFT; defining $f_{\mathrm{peak}}(i)$ as the maximizing (non-DC) frequency, we record $\pi_f(i):=\mathbf{1}\{f_{\mathrm{peak}}(i)\in\Cf\}$ and summarize peak occupancy on proxy sets by averages of $\pi_f$. Together with structural proxy coverage (below), these local descriptors form a per-tile \emph{spatial-spectral feature vector} used to construct the KA regularizer.

\subsection{Feature-conditioned score regularization}
\noindent While the matched-subspace statistic $S_{\mathrm{pre}}$ effectively isolates flow in Gaussian clutter, highly structured artifacts can still elevate false-alarm rates in the extreme right tail. To systematically suppress these artifacts, we introduce a feature-conditioned regularization layer. While we conceptually view this penalty as a prior-odds modifier in log-score space, the construction is deterministic, using local spectral features as auxiliary statistics rather than producing a calibrated probability output.

\paragraph{Matched-subspace statistic as a whitened subspace detector.}
Fix tile $i$ and consider a single slow-time snapshot $x\in\mathbb{C}^{L_t}$.
Under a Gaussian clutter-plus-noise model,
\[
  H_0:\ x \sim \mathcal{CN}(0,R_i),\qquad
  H_1:\ x = A_f s + n,\ \ s\sim \mathcal{CN}(0,\sigma_s^2 I),\ \ n\sim \mathcal{CN}(0,R_i),
\]
where the columns of $A_f$ span the flow subspace (equivalently the range of $\Pf$).
After whitening $y=R_i^{-1/2}x$, the GLRT for $\sigma_s^2>0$ versus $\sigma_s^2=0$ is monotone with the whitened subspace energy
\[
  T(x)=x\Hh R_i^{-1/2}\Pf R_i^{-1/2}x = y\Hh \Pf\, y,
\]
which is a standard matched-subspace / adaptive energy detector. \cite{KayDetection,ScharfFriedlanderMatchedSubspace}
Averaging $T(x)$ over the $K$ local snapshots yields our pre-KA tile statistic $S_{\mathrm{stap,pre}}(i)$.

\paragraph{Local spatial-spectral feature vector.}
For each tile $i$, we define a feature vector $\phi_i\in\R^3$ that summarizes local spectral kinematics and structural proxy support:
\[
  \phi_i :=
  \begin{bmatrix}
    m_{\mathrm{alias}}(i)\\
    r_g(i)\\
    c_f(i)
  \end{bmatrix},
\]
where $m_{\mathrm{alias}}(i)=\log\frac{E_a(i)+\epsilon}{E_f(i)+\epsilon}$ is the alias-to-flow energy ratio, $r_g(i)=E_g(i)/E_{\mathrm{tot}}(i)$ is the guard fraction, and $c_f(i)$ is a deterministic flow-proxy coverage fraction defined below.

\paragraph{Feature-conditioned shrinkage (log-score view).}
We implement knowledge aid via a bounded weight $w(\phi_i)\in(0,1]$ applied in score space:
\[
  S_{\mathrm{stap,post}}(i)=w(\phi_i)\,S_{\mathrm{stap,pre}}(i),\qquad w(\phi_i)\in(0,1].
\]
Equivalently, define a scale factor $\gamma(\phi_i):=1/w(\phi_i)\geq 1$ and write
\[
  S_{\mathrm{stap,post}}(i)=\frac{S_{\mathrm{stap,pre}}(i)}{\gamma(\phi_i)}.
\]
For brevity, we refer to $S_{\mathrm{stap,pre}}$ and $S_{\mathrm{stap,post}}$ as $S_{\mathrm{pre}}$ and $S_{\mathrm{post}}$ when discussing the feature-conditioned penalty and in figure captions.
Equivalently, for $S_{\mathrm{pre}}(i)>0$ define $T_{\mathrm{pre}}(i):=\log S_{\mathrm{pre}}(i)$ and $T_{\mathrm{post}}(i):=\log S_{\mathrm{post}}(i)$, giving
\[
  T_{\mathrm{post}}(i)=T_{\mathrm{pre}}(i)+\log w(\phi_i).
\]
We use this additive log-score form as an \emph{interpretive lens}: it mirrors adding a feature-dependent penalty term to a log-likelihood score, but the construction is deterministic and uses $\phi_i$ as auxiliary statistics rather than producing calibrated probabilities. We constrain it to be shrink-only by enforcing $w(\phi_i)\le 1$. For numerical stability in plots we display $\log_{10}(S+\epsilon)$ with $\epsilon>0$.

\paragraph{Feature-space regimes.}
For auditability and conservative behavior, we partition the feature space into three regimes that control the strength of the prior penalty:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{Uninformative regime $\mathcal{R}_0$.} If sample support is insufficient, band discretization is invalid, or risk features are effectively flat, the prior defaults to uninformative: $w(\phi_i)\equiv 1$, so $S_{\mathrm{stap,post}}=S_{\mathrm{stap,pre}}$.
  \item \textbf{Guarded regularization regime $\mathcal{R}_1$.} If features indicate uncertainty or leakage (e.g.\ elevated guard fractions or moderate alias ratios) but do not satisfy stronger actionability evidence, we apply a bounded penalty $w(\phi_i)\in[w_{\min},1]$ while preserving a protected high-confidence set.
  \item \textbf{Actionable alias-dominance regime $\mathcal{R}_2$ (research-only).} If features indicate alias-dominant structure and are \emph{actionable} in the sense that they explain high-score background-tail events under proxy sets, we permit a stronger but still bounded penalty, while maintaining shrink-only and protected-set invariance.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Proxy sets (label-free) and protected-set invariance.}
When ground-truth $H_1/H_0$ labels are unavailable, we define proxy sets deterministically from a structural proxy (e.g.\ $\mathrm{PD}^{\uparrow}$ or simulator geometry). When using $\mathrm{PD}^{\uparrow}$, we define a high-quantile flow proxy mask
\[
  M_{\mathrm{flow}}(y,x):=\mathbf{1}\{\mathrm{PD}^{\uparrow}(y,x)\ge Q_{q_{\mathrm{flow}}}(\mathrm{PD}^{\uparrow})\},
\]
a per-tile flow coverage
\[
  c_f(i):=\frac{1}{|\Omega_i|}\sum_{(y,x)\in\Omega_i} M_{\mathrm{flow}}(y,x),
\]
and background/flow proxy sets
\[
  \mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{bg}}:=\{i:\ c_f(i)\le c_{\mathrm{bg}}\},\qquad
  \mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{flow}}:=\{i:\ c_f(i)\ge c_{\mathrm{flow}}\}.
\]
To enforce protected-set invariance, we define a protected set $\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{prot}}$ that includes high-confidence flow-proxy tiles (and optionally extreme-score tiles) and restrict penalization to a fixed candidate set of non-protected tiles in an uncertain score window; outside this candidate set we set $w(\phi_i)\equiv 1$.
Region membership ($\phi_i\in\mathcal{R}_0/\mathcal{R}_1/\mathcal{R}_2$) is determined by deterministic integrity checks and by label-free actionability evidence that risk features are associated with high-score background tails (\SuppOrApp{app:ka-contract-theory}).

\paragraph{Penalty mapping (summary).}
Let $S_{\mathrm{pre}}(i)$ denote the (right-tail) pre-KA tile score and let $m_{\mathrm{risk}}(i)$ denote the chosen risk feature (typically $m_{\mathrm{alias}}(i)$; a guard-risk lane may substitute $m_{\mathrm{guard}}$ when guard dominates).
Using frozen design quantiles $q_{\mathrm{lo}},q_{\mathrm{hi}},q_{\mathrm{risk}}$, we define a candidate set
\[
  \mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{cand}} := \Bigl\{i\notin\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{prot}}:\ Q_{q_{\mathrm{lo}}}(S_{\mathrm{pre}})\le S_{\mathrm{pre}}(i)\le Q_{q_{\mathrm{hi}}}(S_{\mathrm{pre}})\Bigr\},
\]
set a background-proxy risk threshold $\tau_{\mathrm{risk}}:=Q_{q_{\mathrm{risk}}}(m_{\mathrm{risk}}\mid \mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{bg}})$, and gate tiles by
\[
  g(i)=\mathbf{1}\{i\in\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{cand}},\ m_{\mathrm{risk}}(i)\ge \tau_{\mathrm{risk}}\}.
\]
On gated tiles we apply a bounded shrink factor
\[
  w(i)=\max\!\left(w_{\min},\exp\!\left(-k\,u(i)\right)\right),\quad
  u(i)=\max\!\left(0,\frac{m_{\mathrm{risk}}(i)-\tau_{\mathrm{risk}}}{\mathrm{MAD}(m_{\mathrm{risk}}\mid\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{bg}})+\epsilon}\right),
\]
and set $w(i)=1$ for $g(i)=0$ and for all $i\in\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{prot}}$. This yields $\gamma(i)=1/w(i)\ge 1$ and $S_{\mathrm{post}}(i)=S_{\mathrm{pre}}(i)/\gamma(i)$.

\begin{lemma}[Shrink-only monotonicity]
\label{lem:shrink_only_monotonicity}
Let $w(i)\in(0,1]$ and define $S_{\mathrm{post}}(i)=w(i)\,S_{\mathrm{pre}}(i)$. For any threshold $\tau$,
\[
  \{S_{\mathrm{post}}\ge\tau\}\subseteq \{S_{\mathrm{pre}}\ge\tau\}.
\]
Consequently, on any subset $A$ (e.g.\ $H_0$), the tail probability cannot increase at fixed $\tau$:
\[
  \mathbb{P}\{S_{\mathrm{post}}\ge\tau\mid A\}\le \mathbb{P}\{S_{\mathrm{pre}}\ge\tau\mid A\}.
\]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
If $S_{\mathrm{post}}(i)\ge\tau$, then $S_{\mathrm{pre}}(i)=S_{\mathrm{post}}(i)/w(i)\ge\tau$ since $w(i)\le 1$.
\end{proof}
When reporting strict low-FPR operating points where $\tau$ is reselected post-KA to satisfy $\mathbb{P}\{S_{\mathrm{post}}\ge\tau\mid H_0\}=\alpha$, changes in TPR reflect background-tail compression despite scores never increasing.

\subsection{Algorithmic integrity and deterministic safeguards}
To make the architecture auditable and to avoid leakage or discretization artifacts masquerading as performance gains, we enforce deterministic, label-free safeguards:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{Conditional execution as a decision-rule variant.} To reduce latency, STAP can be skipped outside a flow-proxy region and replaced by the baseline score there. Because this changes the overall decision rule, any conditional-execution reporting is treated as a distinct algorithmic variant and is accompanied by leakage controls (same-window vs.\ disjoint-window vs.\ randomized proxy masks).
  \item \textbf{Short-ensemble band discretization sanity.} For extremely short ensembles (e.g.\ $N\in\{5,9,17\}$), the coarse frequency resolution $\Delta f=\mathrm{PRF}/N$ can cause requested bands to collapse or overlap after discretization. If the discretized flow and alias bands are empty, DC-only, or overlapping (violating orthogonality in the DFT-bin construction), we force $\phi_i\in\mathcal{R}_0$ (uninformative prior; $w\equiv 1$) with an explicit reason.
  \item \textbf{Sample-support and tail-sizing constraints.} Tail-association metrics are defined on a background-tail subset and become unstable when sample support is insufficient. We use a size-aware background-tail fraction $q_{\mathrm{tail}}^\star=\max(q_{\mathrm{tail}}, n_{\mathrm{tail,min}}/n_{\mathrm{bg}})$ and refuse to enter $\mathcal{R}_2$ (actionable prior) when tail sizes or proxy supports fall below deterministic floors.
  \item \textbf{Proxy-mask circularity control.} Proxy masks are used only for label-free band-energy aggregation, candidate/protected-set definitions, and conditional execution. Quantitative ROC claims are restricted to regimes with independent $H_1/H_0$ labels (simulator truth or B-mode structural geometry).
\end{itemize}

For auditability, we log the selected feature-space region and explicit reason labels so that ``KA off'' ($w\equiv 1$) is an expected success mode under flat or non-actionable evidence. \SuppOrApp{app:ka-contract-theory} provides modality-agnostic sufficient conditions and additional sentinels used to interpret when the KA prior should be active or inert.

\section{Experimental Design}
\label{sec:experimental-design}

\subsection{Fixed-profile protocol and operating-point reporting}
\label{sec:eval}
All hyperparameters (tile geometry, aperture, band definitions, conditioning targets, and prior thresholds) are fixed per regime before reporting results. Detection is evaluated in the right tail at fixed FPR targets:
\[
  \mathbb{P}\{S\ge \tau_\alpha\mid H_0\}=\alpha,\qquad
  \mathrm{TPR}(\alpha)=\mathbb{P}\{S\ge \tau_\alpha\mid H_1\}.
\]
For each experiment, we report the supported floor $fpr_{\min}=1/n_{\mathrm{neg}}$ and bootstrap uncertainty at the natural unit of variation (windows or frames, depending on regime). These choices enforce a strict low-FPR operating discipline and avoid over-interpreting unsupported tail points.

\subsection{Integrity checks and deterministic sentinels}

This work is a reproducible processing pipeline (decode $\rightarrow$ baseline $\rightarrow$ STAP score $\rightarrow$ spatial-spectral features $\rightarrow$ optional KA regularization). Several practical pathologies can invalidate tail-rate reporting or spuriously activate the KA layer (e.g.\ missing acquisition metadata, coarse short-ensemble band discretization, proxy-mask circularity, or compute-gating leakage). We therefore enforce deterministic, label-free integrity checks that either (i) record provenance and proceed conservatively or (ii) force $\phi_i\in\mathcal{R}_0$ (uninformative prior; $w\equiv 1$) with an explicit reason label. A concise checklist (and where each sentinel is logged) is provided in \SuppOrApp{app:failure-modes}.

\subsection{Regimes and claim scope}
\label{sec:results-overview}

Claims are stratified by regime to separate quantitative detection efficacy from label-free instrumentation.

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{Brain-k-Wave simulations (labeled).} Across Brain-OpenSkull / Brain-AliasContract / Brain-SkullOR regimes, the fixed profile yields a nontrivial right-tail shoulder in the primary detector scores (baseline power Doppler and matched-subspace STAP; Notation), with the strongest low-FPR gains in the alias-augmented Brain-AliasContract and skull/OR-inspired Brain-SkullOR regimes (Table~\ref{tab:brain_kwave_vnext_baselines}).
  \item \textbf{Phantom acquisitions (structurally labeled Gammex flow phantom).} We decode raw beamformer outputs, derive B-mode-only tube masks (flow/background), benchmark conventional short-ensemble Doppler baselines, and report structural-label low-FPR operating points and within-ensemble motion ladders.
  \item \textbf{ULM Zenodo 7883227 (real kHz IQ).} We report label-free motion-robustness curves on beamformed IQ blocks using controlled rigid/nonrigid motion injection, with fairness alignment and degeneracy checks (no task ROC).
  \item \textbf{Supplementary analyses.} Additional label-free audits (e.g.\ Shin Fig3 real IQ; PD-only whole-brain fUS) and diagnostic panels are provided in \SuppOrApp{app:supp-results}.
\end{itemize}

\section{Results}

\paragraph{Results roadmap.}
We first report labeled Brain-* simulation results at ultra-low FPR, then structurally labeled Gammex flow phantom results (including motion ladders), and finally label-free motion-robustness evidence on real kHz IQ (ULM 7883227). Additional dataset audits and diagnostic panels are provided in \SuppOrApp{app:supp-results}.

\paragraph{Scope and low-FPR operating protocol.}
Quantitative detection performance is reported on labeled Brain-* simulations and on structurally labeled phantom regimes (Gammex flow phantom sequences; labels derived from B-mode geometry) using the strict low-FPR operating protocol of Section~\ref{sec:eval} at ultra-low FPR. Real kHz IQ datasets are used for instrumentation (band-energy integrity checks and label-free motion-robustness proxies), not task ROC. Conditional execution is a decision-rule variant (compute gating) that uses a flow-proxy mask; leakage ablations and additional diagnostics are reported in \SuppOrApp{app:supp-results}.

\subsection{Phantom acquisitions (Gammex flow phantom): structural ROC and within-ensemble motion ladders}

\paragraph{Dataset and acquisition parameters.}
We use the open-access Doppler Twinkling Artifact database \cite{LeonovTwinklingDD2021}, which provides complex beamformer output from phantom acquisitions on a clinical scanner. Each cine frame contains a contemporaneous B-mode image and a short-ensemble color-flow (CFM) slow-time block with $N\in\{5,9,17\}$ pulses. Because Doppler PRF is not reliably available in the provided metadata for these acquisitions, all Twinkling experiments treat PRF as an explicit experimental assumption frozen per sequence family (listed in \SuppOrApp{app:twinkling_prf_decode}) and enforce band-discretization sanity checks that force $\phi_i\in\mathcal{R}_0$ when discretized Pf/Pa bands are empty or overlapping (Section~\ref{sec:ka-contract-v2}). \paragraph{B-mode-only structural tube masks (independent $H_1/H_0$).}
For the Gammex flow phantom sequences we derive evaluation masks from B-mode structure only (no Doppler/STAP outputs). Using acquisition geometry metadata that specifies the CFM region-of-interest (ROI) within the B-mode grid (beam and sample offsets and any beam subsampling), we map the B-mode ROI onto the CFM grid and segment the tube lumen on a median log-envelope reference image. This yields (i) a conservative lumen (flow) mask, eroded to avoid wall pixels and (ii) a background mask that excludes a guard band around the lumen and bright reflectors. Mask provenance and QC metrics are recorded with each experiment; Figure~\ref{fig:twinkling_gammex_masks} shows representative overlays. These structural labels support a defensible phantom ROC.

\subsection{ULM Zenodo 7883227 (real kHz IQ): baseline calibration and nonrigid motion robustness}
\label{sec:ulm7883227}

\paragraph{Dataset and loader.}
We use a real kHz IQ dataset released for ultrasound localization microscopy benchmarking \cite{ChavignonZenodo7883227,HeilesULMBenchmark2021} and distributed as HDF5 blocks with accompanying acquisition metadata. Each block contains a dataset \texttt{iq} of shape $(2,H,W,T)=(2,256,180,800)$ float32 representing I/Q over $T=800$ slow-time frames. We load blocks on demand and return complex IQ as a cube of shape $(T,H,W)$ (reproduction details in \SuppOrApp{app:repro}).

\paragraph{One-time label-free baseline selection.}
For ULM we select a single MC--SVD energy-fraction cutoff by a one-time label-free sweep on blocks 1--3 over frames $0{:}128$, and then freeze it for all subsequent ULM motion experiments. The sweep uses baseline-only runs and relies on label-free integrity and stability criteria (no task labels); it yields a frozen energy fraction $e=0.975$ (full sweep details in \SuppOrApp{app:repro}).

\paragraph{Nonrigid motion injection and fairness alignment ablation.}
We evaluate motion robustness by injecting controlled motion directly into the beamformed IQ cube and re-running baseline+STAP under a frozen ULM profile (PRF $=1000$ Hz; $L_t=64$; tiles $8\times 8$; stride 3; SCM covariance; diagonal loading $\lambda=0.07$; baseline MC--SVD with frozen $e=0.975$). Because motion is injected at the beamformed IQ stage, this test stresses post-beamforming robustness to residual motion mismatch; it does not model channel-domain decorrelation or skull-induced phase-aberration physics. Motion ladders span two nonrigid families: \texttt{brainlike} (rigid random-walk translation plus a smooth, depth-decaying elastic warp with temporally correlated coefficients and micro-jitter) and \texttt{elastic} (nonrigid-only; rigid and micro-jitter disabled). For each amplitude we compute label-free robustness endpoints on the baseline score $S_{\mathrm{base}}$ and the matched-subspace STAP score $S_{\mathrm{stap,pre}}$ relative to the no-motion reference under three \emph{common} alignment modes (none / common B-mode / injected GT proxy) plus a diagnostic per-method ``self'' alignment. We additionally report degeneracy checks (std ratios, top-$k$ overlap, and tail quantile ratios) to rule out ``stability by collapse.'' Figure~\ref{fig:ulm_brainlike_e975} shows the main \texttt{brainlike} sweep; additional motion variants are provided in \SuppOrApp{app:supp-results}.

\emph{Endpoint definition.} For a score map $S_{\mathrm{meth}}(a)$ at motion amplitude $a$ and the corresponding no-motion reference $S_{\mathrm{meth}}(0)$ (same block), we compute $\rho_{\mathrm{meth}}(a)$ as the Pearson correlation between the aligned maps on a central crop $\Omega_a$ that excludes a margin of $\lceil a\rceil+2$ pixels to avoid boundary artifacts from warping. Correlations are computed directly on the exported right-tail scores (no log transform) and use the same crop/alignment for baseline and STAP in the ``common'' alignment modes.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\subsection*{A) Robustness in the ultra-low-FPR tail}
This work adapts space--time adaptive processing (STAP), a foundational approach in radar target detection under heavy clutter, to functional ultrasound (fUS) slow-time flow detection. Under the strict low-FPR operating protocol (Section~\ref{sec:eval}), we find that common global or semi-global clutter suppressors (MC--SVD, RPCA, HOSVD) can lose sensitivity in the extreme right tail, while the proposed tile-based matched-subspace STAP score preserves a distinct, non-saturated shoulder at ultra-low FPR. This behavior is demonstrated on labeled k-Wave simulations and structurally labeled \emph{in vitro} phantoms, where flow/background masks are defined independently from Doppler/STAP outputs via simulator truth or B-mode geometry. The within-ensemble motion ladders on phantom acquisitions further indicate that STAP retains detection stability under controlled per-shot motion corruption, consistent with its reliance on localized covariance rather than global low-rank structure.

\subsection*{B) Knowledge-aided prior as a fail-safe}
The optional knowledge-aided (KA) layer is designed as a conservative safety regularizer rather than an unconstrained signal amplifier. It acts as a shrink-only prior penalty with an auditable (region, reason) output: when spatial-spectral evidence is flat or non-actionable, the prior defaults to $\mathcal{R}_0$ (uninformative; $w\equiv 1$) and leaves the pre-KA detector score unchanged. When the prior lies in $\mathcal{R}_1/\mathcal{R}_2$, it enforces protected-set invariance and applies only non-increasing shrink on high-risk regions, so that ``KA does nothing'' is an intended success mode out of regime. This posture prioritizes transparent regime diagnostics and avoids score inflation in regimes where spectral evidence cannot justify aggressive intervention.

\subsection*{C) Data limitations and the phase-history barrier}
A key limitation in evaluating advanced Doppler-side covariance methods is field-wide data availability: many open-access \emph{in vivo} fUS datasets are temporally downsampled, post-filtered, or released only as power-Doppler equivalents, which can irreversibly destroy the high-rate complex phase history needed for spatiotemporal covariance estimation and band-energy integrity checks. Accordingly, while we report real-data instrumentation (band-energy integrity, prior-regime behavior, and label-free stability under controlled motion corruption), comprehensive \emph{in vivo} ROC evaluation of Doppler-side STAP/KA requires high-PRF raw IQ acquisitions prior to proprietary clutter suppression, with reliable metadata and task/landmark structure to define independent evaluation labels.

\section{Conclusion and future work}
This study establishes a deterministic, auditable KA-STAP framework for fUS slow-time flow detection under a tune-once-then-freeze discipline. Using a strict low-FPR operating protocol and independent structural labels where available, we show that localized matched-subspace STAP can preserve separability in regimes where conventional low-rank baselines degrade in the extreme tail, while the KA spatial-spectral prior provides shrink-only, protected-set-preserving risk control with explicit out-of-regime behavior.

Future work is to (i) evaluate Doppler-side STAP/KA on pre-registered, labeled raw-IQ \emph{in vivo} datasets that preserve phase history and support independent task/landmark labels, (ii) reduce latency via hardware-aware implementations of the batched covariance and band-energy kernels toward real-time deployment, and (iii) explore compact, frozen learned regularizers only when they can be constrained to preserve the shrink-only auditability and low-FPR reporting guarantees established here.

\section{Practical Band Design for Brain fUS (PRF 1.5--5 kHz, T 8--16)}
\label{sec:band-geometry}

We provide practical band guidelines for selecting Pf/guard/Pa with explicit physical anchors.

For brain fUS at center frequencies $f_0 \approx 6$--$15$ MHz and PRF $\approx 1.5$--$5$ kHz \cite{Mace2013,Rabut2020,Imbault2017,Urban2022,LampeFUSBrain}:

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item Microvascular brain flows (arterioles/venules) in humans and rodents are typically in the range $v \approx 3$--$20$ mm/s for the vessels that dominate functional responses \cite{Imbault2017,Urban2022}. At $f_0=7.5$ MHz and $c\approx 1540$ m/s, this corresponds to Doppler shifts $f_D\approx 30$--$200$ Hz.
  \item Low--frequency tissue motion (respiration, cardiac pulsation) produces strong spectral content below $\approx 20$ Hz; this should be contained in Po and largely removed by high--pass filtering and/or SVD.
  \item Large--vessel flows (e.g.\ MCA--like arteries) reach $v\approx 0.3$--$0.8$ m/s, corresponding to $f_D$ in the kHz range \cite{TCDVelocities,TCDOptimalFlow}. Depending on PRF and angle, a portion of this energy appears in high Doppler bands and can alias into the $>$400 Hz region corresponding to Pa \cite{ICDFUS2021,MobileFUS2024}.
\end{itemize}

Thus a reasonable fixed band design for brain fUS with $T\approx 8$--$16$ and PRF $\approx 1.5$--$3$ kHz is:

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item Flow band $\Cf$: $[30,250]$ Hz --- microvascular flows $\approx 3$--$20$ mm/s.
  \item Guard band $\Cg$: $[250,400]$ Hz --- protects against band--edge leakage.
  \item Alias band $\Ca$: $[400, \text{Nyquist}]$ Hz --- high Doppler where large--vessel/aliased flows and certain motion artifacts live.
\end{itemize}

In discrete terms, for $T=8$ at PRF=1.5 kHz (bin spacing $187.5$ Hz), this maps to:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item $\Pf$ on first off--DC bin (with DPSS leakage covering $\approx 30$--$250$ Hz),
  \item guard around the second bin,
  \item $\Pa$ on higher bins with bin--level guard as per BG--1/BG--2.
\end{itemize}

\section{PD-only KA-prior summary panel (hemodynamic): band-energy statistics and aggregation}
\label{app:pdonly-dashboard}

This appendix specifies a PD-only instantiation of the KA spatial-spectral prior and the associated summary-panel aggregation used for systematic plane sweeps (e.g.\ Mac{\'e}/Urban whole-brain mouse fUS). The key discipline is unchanged: \emph{runtime} prior selection is label-free (proxy sets), while atlas-defined $H_1/H_0$ labels are used only \emph{offline} for descriptive reporting and are never used as prior inputs.

\subsection*{A) PD-only band-energy feature definitions (math)}
\paragraph{PD movie and tiling.}
Fix one coronal plane (one ``configuration'') with a PD movie
\[
P[t,y,x]\in\R_{\ge 0},\qquad t=0,\dots,T-1,
\]
sampled at rate $f_s=1/dt$ (Mac{\'e}: $f_s\approx 10$~Hz). Tile each frame into overlapping spatial tiles $\Omega_i$ of size $h\times w$ (Mac{\'e}: $8\times 8$) with stride $s$ (Mac{\'e}: $s=3$).

\paragraph{Tile PD time series.}
Define the tile-averaged PD time series
\[
p_i[t] := \frac{1}{|\Omega_i|}\sum_{(y,x)\in\Omega_i} P[t,y,x].
\]
For robustness across tiles, we optionally use a log-stabilized, mean-removed series
\[
u_i[t] := \log(p_i[t]+\epsilon_{\mathrm{pd}}),\qquad
\tilde u_i[t] := u_i[t]-\frac{1}{T}\sum_{t'=0}^{T-1} u_i[t'].
\]

\paragraph{Hankel snapshots and whitening.}
Fix a slow-time aperture $L_t$ (tens of frames in PD-only). Define $K=T-L_t+1$ and Hankel snapshots
\[
x_{i,k} :=
\begin{bmatrix}
\tilde u_i[k]\\
\tilde u_i[k+1]\\
\vdots\\
\tilde u_i[k+L_t-1]
\end{bmatrix}\in\R^{L_t},\qquad k=0,\dots,K-1.
\]
Estimate a per-tile covariance (SCM or robust variant)
\[
\wh R_i := \frac{1}{K}\sum_{k=0}^{K-1} x_{i,k}x_{i,k}\Hh,
\]
apply conditioning/diagonal loading to obtain $R_i$, and whiten
\[
y_{i,k} := R_i^{-1/2}x_{i,k}.
\]
We pool whitened snapshots to a single per-tile slow-time vector
\[
z_i := \frac{1}{K}\sum_{k=0}^{K-1} y_{i,k}\in\R^{L_t}.
\]

\paragraph{Hemodynamic band projectors and energies.}
Let $\Cf,\Cg,\Ca$ denote Pf/guard/Pa hemodynamic bands (Mac{\'e}: $\Cf=[0,0.5]$~Hz, $\Cg=(0.5,1.0]$~Hz, $\Ca=(1.0,3.0]$~Hz). Build fixed temporal projectors $(\Pf,\Pg,\Pa)$ onto the span of discrete Fourier tones whose frequencies lie in these bands, and define $\Po := I-\Pf-\Pg-\Pa$ (``other''/out-of-band). Define band energies
\[
E_f(i) := z_i\Hh \Pf z_i,\quad
E_g(i) := z_i\Hh \Pg z_i,\quad
E_a(i) := z_i\Hh \Pa z_i,\quad
E_o(i) := z_i\Hh \Po z_i,
\]
and $E_{\mathrm{tot}}(i) := E_f+E_g+E_a+E_o+\epsilon$.

\paragraph{Risk metrics and Pf-peak statistics.}
We use the same KA feature primitives as in the Doppler regime:
\[
m_{\mathrm{alias}}(i) := \log\frac{E_a(i)+\epsilon}{E_f(i)+\epsilon},\qquad
m_{\mathrm{guard}}(i) := \log\frac{E_g(i)+\epsilon}{E_f(i)+\epsilon},\qquad
r_g(i) := \frac{E_g(i)}{E_{\mathrm{tot}}(i)}.
\]
Define a whitened periodogram of the pooled vector $z_i$,
\[
\wh z_i[n] := \sum_{\ell=0}^{L_t-1} z_i[\ell]\,e^{-2\pi i n\ell/L_t},\qquad
S_i[n] := |\wh z_i[n]|^2,\qquad n=0,\dots,\lfloor L_t/2\rfloor,
\]
and the (non-DC) peak frequency $f_{\mathrm{peak}}(i)=\frac{n_{\mathrm{peak}}}{L_t dt}$ where
$n_{\mathrm{peak}}(i):=\arg\max_{n\ge 1} S_i[n]$. The Pf-peak indicator and occupancy on a set $\mathcal{T}$ are
\[
\pi_f(i) := \mathbf{1}\{f_{\mathrm{peak}}(i)\in\Cf\},\qquad
\mathrm{PfPeakFrac}(\mathcal{T}) := \frac{1}{|\mathcal{T}|}\sum_{i\in\mathcal{T}}\pi_f(i).
\]

\subsection*{B) PD-only proxy sets and prior inputs (math)}
The PD-only prior uses label-free proxy sets based on a PD-derived flow-coverage proxy, analogous to the IQ flow-mask coverage $c_f$.
Let $\bar P[y,x] := \frac{1}{T}\sum_t P[t,y,x]$ be the mean PD image (optionally restricted to a brain mask). Define a high-quantile flow proxy mask
\[
M_{\mathrm{flow}}(y,x) := \mathbf{1}\{\bar P[y,x]\ge Q_{q_{\mathrm{flow}}}(\bar P)\},
\]
and per-tile flow coverage
\[
c_f(i) := \frac{1}{|\Omega_i|}\sum_{(y,x)\in\Omega_i} M_{\mathrm{flow}}(y,x)\in[0,1].
\]
With frozen design thresholds $c_{\mathrm{bg}}$ and $c_{\mathrm{flow}}$ (typical $c_{\mathrm{bg}}\approx 0.05$, $c_{\mathrm{flow}}\approx 0.20$), define
\[
\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{bg}} := \{i:\ c_f(i)\le c_{\mathrm{bg}}\},\quad
\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{flow}} := \{i:\ c_f(i)\ge c_{\mathrm{flow}}\},\quad
\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{nonbg}} := \{i:\ c_f(i)>c_{\mathrm{bg}}\}.
\]
If $\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{flow}}$ is too small (insufficient proxy support), a conservative fallback is to define proxy sets via tilewise mean-$\bar P$ quantiles and record an explicit ``proxy\_fallback'' reason code.

\subsection*{C) Prior-regime selection for PD-only (math)}
\paragraph{Baseline score for PD-only prior.}
For Mac{\'e}, the baseline scalar detector is PD-z on tile time series: choose a baseline segment $B$ (e.g.\ first third of frames) and define
\[
s_{\mathrm{base}}(i) := \max_{t\notin B} \frac{u_i[t]-\mu_i^{(B)}}{\sigma_i^{(B)}+\epsilon_z},
\]
with $\mu_i^{(B)},\sigma_i^{(B)}$ the mean and standard deviation of $u_i[t]$ over $t\in B$. Higher $s_{\mathrm{base}}$ means more activation.

\paragraph{Risk-mode selection and integrity checks.}
The PD-only prior follows the same posture as the Doppler-IQ prior:
if risk statistics are flat (e.g.\ $\mathrm{IQR}(m_{\mathrm{alias}}\mid\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{bg}})$ too small) or proxy support is insufficient, we default to the uninformative regime ($\mathcal{R}_0$; $w\equiv 1$) with an explicit reason label (e.g.\ \texttt{alias\_metric\_flat}, \texttt{insufficient\_bg\_samples}). If guard dominates (e.g.\ $Q_{0.90}(r_g\mid\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{bg}})$ above a frozen threshold), we disallow the actionable regime $\mathcal{R}_2$ and either remain in a guard-risk safety lane ($\mathcal{R}_1$; requiring tail association of $r_g$) or default to $\mathcal{R}_0$ as out of regime (e.g.\ \texttt{guard\_extreme\_out\_of\_regime}).

\paragraph{Actionability checks (actionable regime $\mathcal{R}_2$, label-free).}
The actionable regime $\mathcal{R}_2$ (research-only eligibility; reporting-only) requires additional evidence that risk statistics explain high-score background tails. In the selected risk mode ($m_{\mathrm{risk}}=m_{\mathrm{alias}}$ or $m_{\mathrm{guard}}$), define
\[
\Delta_{bf} := \operatorname{median}(m_{\mathrm{risk}}\mid\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{bg}})-
               \operatorname{median}(m_{\mathrm{risk}}\mid\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{flow}}),
\]
and within $\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{bg}}$ define a tail subset $\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{tail}}$ as the top $1\%$ of $s_{\mathrm{base}}$ and a mid subset $\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{mid}}$ as a central quantile band; require
\[
\Delta_{\mathrm{tail}} := \operatorname{median}(m_{\mathrm{risk}}\mid\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{tail}})-
                          \operatorname{median}(m_{\mathrm{risk}}\mid\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{mid}})
                          \ge \Delta_{\mathrm{tail},\min}.
\]
Finally, we enforce a Pf-realization eligibility condition for $\mathcal{R}_2$:
\[
\mathrm{PfPeakFrac}(\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{flow}})\ge p_{f,\min}.
\]
If these checks fail, the prior remains in $\mathcal{R}_0$ or $\mathcal{R}_1$ (guarded only) and does not enter $\mathcal{R}_2$.

\paragraph{Coverage sentinels.}
As in the Doppler-IQ prior, penalization must be neither inert nor global: after defining protected/candidate sets (below), the penalized fraction
\[
p_{\mathrm{shrink}} := \mathbb{P}\{g=1\mid \mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{cand}}\}
\]
must lie in frozen bounds (e.g.\ $p_{\min}\le p_{\mathrm{shrink}}\le p_{\max}$), otherwise we default to $\mathcal{R}_0$ ($w\equiv 1$) with reason \texttt{coverage\_out\_of\_bounds}.

\subsection*{D) PD-only shrink-only prior mapping (optional; summary-panel parity)}
The PD-only instantiation supports both a hard suppression used in the Mac{\'e} systematic-sweep reporting and a soft shrink mapping for summary-panel parity.
Define a protected set $\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{prot}}$ (tiles with high flow coverage and/or very high baseline scores) and a candidate set $\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{cand}}$ (non-protected tiles in an uncertain score window). Using a background-calibrated risk threshold $\tau_{\mathrm{risk}}=Q_{q_{\mathrm{risk}}}(m_{\mathrm{risk}}\mid\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{bg}})$, define the penalization indicator
\[
g(i)=\mathbf{1}\{i\in\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{cand}},\ m_{\mathrm{risk}}(i)\ge\tau_{\mathrm{risk}}\}.
\]
\emph{Hard suppression (Mac{\'e} sweep):} apply the binary indicator $g$ as a hard suppression on positive activations,
\[
s_{\mathrm{KA}}(i)=s_{\mathrm{base}}(i) - g(i)\max(s_{\mathrm{base}}(i),0),
\]
which is equivalent to setting positive penalized tiles to zero while leaving negative scores unchanged. \emph{Soft shrink (summary-panel parity):} set $w(i)=\max(w_{\min},\exp(-k\,u(i)))$ with a robust normalized exceedance $u(i)\ge 0$ and apply $s_{\mathrm{KA}}(i)=s_{\mathrm{base}}(i)-(1-w(i))\max(s_{\mathrm{base}}(i),0)$. In both cases, $g(i)=0$ (and $w(i)=1$) on $\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{prot}}$ enforces the protected-set invariance and $s_{\mathrm{KA}}(i)\le s_{\mathrm{base}}(i)$ enforces shrink-only behavior.

\subsection*{E) Summary-panel schema and aggregation (PD-only)}
For PD-only datasets the summary panel is computed per plane (configuration) and written as a CSV with one row per (scan, plane). Each row records: (i) identifiers and frozen settings (scan/plane, $T$, $f_s$, $L_t$, tile geometry, band edges), (ii) proxy support counts ($|\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{bg}}|$, $|\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{flow}}|$), (iii) prior outputs (region $\in\{\mathcal{R}_0,\mathcal{R}_1,\mathcal{R}_2\}$, reason label, risk\_mode; exported in code as \texttt{state}/\texttt{reason}), (iv) key band-energy statistics ($\mathrm{PfPeakFrac}(\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{flow}})$, $Q_{0.90}(r_g\mid\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{bg}})$, $\mathrm{IQR}(m_{\mathrm{risk}}\mid\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{bg}})$, $\Delta_{bf}$, $\Delta_{\mathrm{tail}}$), and (v) KA coverage/strength when active ($p_{\mathrm{shrink}}$ and scale/shrink quantiles). Atlas-labeled descriptive fields are prefixed \texttt{offline\_} (e.g.\ \texttt{offline\_pf\_peak\_frac\_H1}, \texttt{offline\_pf\_peak\_frac\_H0}, \texttt{offline\_hits\_pre/post}) to make clear that they are not prior inputs.

Summary-panel aggregation mirrors the Doppler-IQ diagnostic panel: histograms of prior regions and (region, reason) pairs, a risk-mode histogram, distributions of key band-energy statistics (Pf peak fraction, guard q90, risk IQR), and (for active planes) shrink coverage/strength summaries. Any atlas-stratified Mac{\'e} plots (e.g.\ PfPeak(VIS/SC/LGd) vs PfPeak(CP/CA/DG), alias separation) are presented as a separate, explicitly offline/descriptive panel.

\subsection*{F) ``Do not overclaim'' messaging (PD-only summary panel)}
When reporting a PD-only prior summary panel, we use the following posture in captions and limitations:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item PD-only band-energy statistics operate on low-rate PD time series and are an auditability check; they do not validate Doppler-side uplift on raw IQ.
  \item The uninformative regime ($\mathcal{R}_0$; $w\equiv 1$) is an intended success outcome when feature evidence is flat or non-actionable; the summary panel is designed to make these cases explicit via reason labels.
  \item Atlas-stratified results on Mac{\'e} are descriptive; prior-positive slices identified by scanning the same dataset are not treated as held-out confirmation.
  \item The actionable regime ($\mathcal{R}_2$) is a reporting discipline requiring additional label-free actionability evidence; $\mathcal{R}_1$ is guarded only and should not be presented as a clinical performance guarantee.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Knowledge-Aided Prior Regime Selection (code defaults)}
The KA prior regime selection is implemented in \path{pipeline/stap/ka_contract_v2.py}. The entry point is \path{evaluate_ka_contract_v2}. It consumes per-tile arrays
$S_{\mathrm{base}}$, $m_{\mathrm{alias}}$, $r_g$, $c_f$, and $\mathrm{PfPeak}$ and returns a JSON-serializable report with fields \path{state} (region), \path{reason}, and a \path{metrics} dictionary. The frozen defaults used throughout this repository are the \path{KaContractV2Config} dataclass defaults:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{Proxy thresholds:} $c_{\mathrm{bg}}=0.05$, $c_{\mathrm{flow}}=0.20$.
  \item \textbf{Candidate/protected score quantiles:} candidate window $q_{\mathrm{lo}}=0.30$, $q_{\mathrm{hi,cand}}=1.0$; optional score-protect quantile $q_{\mathrm{hi,prot}}=0.99999$ (\path{protect_hi_by_score=true}).
  \item \textbf{Risk threshold:} $q_{\mathrm{risk}}=0.90$ applied to $m_{\mathrm{alias}}$ on $\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{bg}}$.
  \item \textbf{Feature integrity (flatness + guard):} $\mathrm{IQR}(m_{\mathrm{alias}}\mid\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{bg}})\ge 0.25$; actionable eligibility disabled if $Q_{0.90}(r_g)>0.25$; ``guard safety'' lane allowed up to $Q_{0.90}(r_g)\le 0.70$ if guard is actionable, requiring $\mathrm{IQR}(r_g\mid\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{bg}})\ge 0.03$ and $\Delta_{\mathrm{tail,guard}}\ge 0.01$.
  \item \textbf{C2 actionability thresholds:} $\Delta_{bf}\ge 0.30$ and $\Delta_{\mathrm{tail}}\ge 0.20$ (computed on $\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{bg}}/\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{flow}}$ proxies as in \ifSTAPSUPPLEMENTONLY the main paper\else Section~\ref{sec:ka-contract-v2}\fi).
  \item \textbf{C2 Pf-realization eligibility:} $\mathrm{PfPeakFrac}(\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{flow}})\ge 0.05$ with $|\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{flow}}|\ge 50$.
  \item \textbf{Coverage bounds:} safety lane $p_{\mathrm{shrink}}\in[0.02,0.25]$ (guard lane $[0.02,0.15]$), actionable lane $p_{\mathrm{shrink}}\in[0.05,0.40]$.
  \item \textbf{Invariance sentinel:} if $\mathrm{IQR}(\log w\mid \text{penalized}) < 0.02$ then return C0 with \path{invariance_no_effect}.
  \item \textbf{Sample support:} $n_{\min}=500$ valid tiles, $n_{\mathrm{bg},\min}=200$ background-proxy tiles, $n_{\mathrm{cand},\min}=200$ candidate tiles, $n_{\mathrm{tail},\min}=20$, $n_{\mathrm{mid},\min}=20$.
\end{itemize}
The implementation exports \path{C0_OFF}, \path{C1_SAFETY}, or \path{C2_ACTIONABLE} (exported as \path{C2_UPLIFT} in current metadata for backward compatibility). These correspond to feature-space regimes $\mathcal{R}_0/\mathcal{R}_1/\mathcal{R}_2$ in the main text. Disable reasons are explicit and frozen in code; common ones include:
\path{missing_inputs}, \path{shape_mismatch}, \path{insufficient_samples}, \path{insufficient_bg_samples},
\path{alias_metric_flat}, \path{guard_metric_missing}, \path{guard_extreme_out_of_regime},
\path{guard_dominates_not_actionable}, \path{pf_peak_flow_below_min}, \path{too_few_candidates},
\path{coverage_out_of_bounds}, and \path{invariance_no_effect}. Successful enablement returns \path{reason=ok}.
For short-ensemble Doppler runs, bundle writers may additionally force C0 with \path{reason=band_sanity_invalid} when discrete band binning yields empty/overlapping Pf/Pa after discretization (see \path{sim/kwave/icube_bundle.py}).

\subsection{Score-space shrink mapping and exported maps}
When score-space KA is enabled, per-tile shrink factors $w(i)\in(0,1]$ are derived from the regime report by \path{derive_score_shrink_v2_tile_scales} in \path{pipeline/stap/ka_contract_v2.py} and applied in score space as
\[
S_{\mathrm{post}}(i)=w(i)\,S_{\mathrm{pre}}(i),\qquad w(i)\in(0,1].
\]
For convenience (and compatibility with legacy \path{pd} arrays), bundles store an equivalent per-pixel \emph{scale} map $\gamma(i)=1/w(i)\ge 1$ as \path{ka_scale_map.npy}, averaged over overlapping tiles and applied only on the union of penalized tiles as
\[
\mathrm{PD}_{\mathrm{post}}(i)=\mathrm{PD}_{\mathrm{pre}}(i)/\gamma(i).
\]
A binary \path{ka_gate_map.npy} stores the penalized region. Protected pixels are enforced by setting $\gamma=1$ on the protected set (flow mask and extreme-score pixels as in \ifSTAPSUPPLEMENTONLY the main paper\else Section~\ref{sec:ka-contract-v2}\fi).

\paragraph{Implementation score conventions.}
Throughout the manuscript, quantitative low-FPR operating points are reported using the right-tail detector score maps intended for labeled ROC and robustness reporting \ifSTAPSUPPLEMENTONLY (see the score taxonomy table in the main paper)\else (Table~\ref{tab:score_taxonomy})\fi. In the exported bundles these are stored as \path{score_base} (baseline power Doppler score) and \path{score_stap_preka} (pre-KA matched-subspace STAP score), with optional baseline comparators \path{score_base_pdlog} and \path{score_base_kasai}. PD-mode scores (\path{score_pd_*.npy}) are legacy artifacts used for proxying/auditing and for PD-only instrumentation regimes; they are not used as the primary detector scores for labeled ROC reporting in this manuscript. When KA is enabled, the exported maps enforce a shrink-only mapping on the chosen score: in this right-tail score space, $S_{\mathrm{post}}=w\,S_{\mathrm{pre}}$ with $w\in(0,1]$; in PD-mode, \path{ka_scale_map.npy} stores $\gamma=1/w\ge 1$ and is applied as $\mathrm{PD}_{\mathrm{post}}=\mathrm{PD}_{\mathrm{pre}}/\gamma$ on penalized regions.

\section{Implementation details (supplementary)}
\label{app:impl-details}

\subsection{RPCA baseline (full)}
The RPCA baseline used in this repository is implemented as \path{sim/kwave/common.py:_baseline_pd_rpca}. It performs a windowed Principal Component Pursuit (PCP) using an inexact augmented Lagrangian method (IALM) on a downsampled Casorati matrix:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item Spatial downsampling: complex average pooling by factor 2 when $\max(H,W)>128$.
  \item Temporal windows: default subwindow length $t_{\mathrm{sub}}=80$ (or shorter if $T<80$); windows are processed sequentially.
  \item Optimization: IALM with at most 80 inner iterations per window (\path{max_iters_inner=min(max_iters,80)}), tolerance $10^{-4}$.
  \item Shrinkage: $\lambda = 1/\sqrt{\max(m,n)}$ when not supplied, where $m=T_w$ and $n=H_dW_d$.
  \item Low-rank step: singular value thresholding using either full SVD or a randomized SVD depending on matrix size, with a per-window rank heuristic $k=\min(8,\max(2,\lfloor T_w/10\rfloor))$.
  \item Output PD: sum of sparse-component energy over windows on the downsampled grid, then nearest-neighbor upsampling to the original resolution.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{HOSVD baseline (full)}
The tensor HOSVD baseline is implemented as \path{sim/kwave/common.py:_baseline_pd_hosvd}. The cube $X\in\mathbb{C}^{T\times H\times W}$ is optionally spatially downsampled (complex average pooling). Mode-0/1/2 SVDs are computed on the unfoldings and ranks are chosen either explicitly (\texttt{--hosvd-ranks}) or by energy fractions (\texttt{--hosvd-energy-fracs}, default $(0.99,0.99,0.99)$). The low-rank approximation is reconstructed by projecting onto the multilinear subspace, and PD is the time-averaged residual energy $\frac{1}{T}\sum_t |X(t)-L(t)|^2$. In the brain baselines used in this repository, the Makefile target \texttt{r4c-replay-hosvd} runs HOSVD with \texttt{--hosvd-spatial-downsample 2} and \texttt{--hosvd-energy-fracs 0.99,0.99,0.99}.

\subsection{STAP temporal core (full)}
For k-Wave pilots and replay bundles, the temporal STAP implementation used for these exported bundles lives in \path{sim/kwave/common.py} (\path{_stap_pd_tile_lcmv} and helpers) and uses the same conceptual decomposition as the main text: Hankelize slow-time tiles, estimate per-tile covariances with a robust estimator (\path{tyler_pca} or Huber), apply diagonal loading with condition-number targets (MVDR load in \path{auto} mode), whiten, and compute band-energy statistics using a fixed Doppler tone grid. The conservative-profile PD fast path and conditional STAP are enabled by environment variables (\path{STAP_FAST_PD_ONLY=1}, \path{STAP_MAX_SNAPSHOTS}, \path{STAP_SNAPSHOT_STRIDE}) and by skipping tiles with zero flow-mask overlap (telemetry key \path{stap_tiles_skipped_flow0} in \path{meta.json}). For reproducible run settings, the Makefile targets and scripts in \path{scripts/} record the exact STAP arguments passed to \path{scripts/replay_stap_from_run.py} (e.g.\ \path{diag_load}, \path{mvdr_auto_kappa}, \path{grid_step_rel}, \path{max_pts}) and export them in each bundle's \path{meta.json}.

\subsection{Covariance blending constraints and KA modes}

We permit anisotropic shrinkage, but only \emph{within} bands. In practice we consider two KA paths:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{Updated (commuting band--scalar).} $Q\Hh Q$ is forced to be bandwise scalar with target gains $(s_f,s_a,s_o)$ (in code: $s_f=s_o\approx 1$, $s_a\approx 1.5$), small mixing, and no off--block priors; the content of $R_0$ beyond $(\Pf,\Pa)$ is ignored.
  \item \textbf{Blend.} A trace--normalized blend $R_\beta = (1-\beta) R_t^{(n)} + \beta R_0^{(n)}$ with a small diagonal lift. In practice $R_0$ is overridden to a band--scalar form (Pf weight 1, Pa weight $\gamma_a>1$, Po weight 1) so the geometry is explicit; $\beta$ is treated as a fixed hyperparameter per regime.
\end{itemize}
Both modes enforce bandwise structure; there are no off--block priors.

\subsection{CB--1: Band--Block Blending}

We construct
\[
R_\beta := (1-\beta)\,\wh R + \beta \bigl(R_{0,f}\oplus R_{0,a}\oplus R_{0,o}\bigr) + \delta I,
\]
where $R_{0,f} = \Pf R_0 \Pf$, etc., for some prior $R_0$. There are no off--block priors; $\beta\in[0,1)$ and $\delta\ge 0$.

Interpretation: this structure makes $Q$ nearly block--diagonal in $(\Po,\Pf,\Pg,\Pa)$ while still allowing bandwise control to realize OC--2.

\subsection{CB--2: Bandwise Spectral Targets}

We choose $(\beta,\delta)$ to enforce OC--2 explicitly:
\[
\lambda_{\min}(Q\Hh Q|_{\Pf}) \in [s_f^2(1-\delta_f), s_f^2(1+\delta_f)],\quad\text{etc.}
\]
with design tolerances $\delta_\bullet\le 0.1$ and separation $\Lambda = s_a/s_f \ge 1.25$.

\subsection{CB--3: Bandwise Condition Number Safeguard}

We constrain bandwise conditioning:
\[
\kappa(R_\beta|_{\Pf}) \le \kappa_f^{\max},\qquad
\kappa(R_\beta|_{\Pa}) \le \kappa_a^{\max},
\]
avoid an incompatibility between conditioning and preserving flow Rayleigh quotients.

\subsection{Localization (restricting KA in score space)}
\label{app:gating}

A global band--constant transform with $Q$ cannot improve ROC if applied everywhere (it is close to a scalar reweighting of BR/GLRT). To create re--ranking, KA must be \emph{localized} to tiles where:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item scores are dominated by alias/undesired components,
  \item positives are unlikely to lie.
\end{itemize}

We define localization based on a combination of alias metrics, flow coverage, depth, PD, and registration. This supplements G--1/G--2 with a generalized alias score $m_{\mathrm{alias}}$ from Section~\ref{sec:regime}.

\subsection{G--1: Alias--dominant tile selection}

Given a baseline tile $z$, compute per--band energies $(E_o,E_f,E_g,E_a)$ and a generalized alias score $m_{\mathrm{alias}}(z)$ satisfying R--2.

\paragraph{Data--adaptive threshold.}
For BG tiles (or their proxy), collect $m_{\mathrm{alias}}(z)$ and define
\[
\tau_{\mathrm{alias}} := Q_{q_{\mathrm{alias}}}\bigl(m_{\mathrm{alias}} \mid H_0\bigr),
\]
where $Q_q$ is the $q$--quantile and $q_{\mathrm{alias}}\in[0.7,0.9]$ is a design choice (typical: $0.8$ or $0.9$).

Define an alias gate
\[
g_{\mathrm{alias}}(z) = \mathbf{1}\left\{ m_{\mathrm{alias}}(z) \ge \tau_{\mathrm{alias}} \right\}.
\]

\paragraph{Band--edge purity.}
Additionally require that guard energy is small relative to Pf+Pa:
\[
\frac{E_g}{E_o + E_f + E_a + E_g} \le \eta_{\text{edge}},\quad
\eta_{\text{edge}}\le 0.1.
\]

Interpretation: this gate selects the top tail of alias--heavy tiles among negatives, while protecting against band--edge contamination. The distribution of $m_{\mathrm{alias}}$ across classes is governed by R--2; empirical validation (Section~\ref{sec:regime}) must show a nontrivial difference between $H_0$ and $H_1$.

\subsection{G--2: Flow coherence protection}

We can optionally compute a flow coherence metric $\mathrm{FPC}$ (first principal component fraction) within the flow band:
\[
\mathrm{FPC} := \frac{\lambda_1(\wh S_f)}{\tr(\wh S_f)},\qquad
\wh S_f := \frac{1}{K}\sum_{k=1}^K u_k u_k\Hh,\quad
u_k := \Pf z^{(k)},
\]
where $z^{(k)}$ are projected slow--time vectors across DPSS tapers or within a local ensemble.

Gate \emph{may} require
\[
\mathrm{FPC}\le c_{\text{pc}},\qquad c_{\text{pc}}\in[0.4,0.6].
\]

Interpretation: flow tiles tend to exhibit higher coherence in Pf; when enabled, we suppress penalization when $\mathrm{FPC}$ is large, thereby reducing positive penalization probability $p_+$. Alias/motion tiles have lower flow coherence and pass this condition. In the current runs this check is typically left off; coverage and depth localization carry most of the class separation.

\subsection{G--2': Flow coverage protection}

Let $c_f$ be the tile--wise flow coverage fraction:
\[
c_f := \frac{\#\{\text{pixels labeled as flow}\}}{\#\{\text{pixels in tile}\}},
\]
using a label--free proxy such as a PD--derived flow mask.

We require
\[
c_f \le c_{f,\max},\qquad c_{f,\max}\in[0.1,0.2],
\]
and optionally $c_f \ge c_{f,\min}$ to avoid pure background noise tiles (typical $c_{f,\min}\approx 0$).

Interpretation: we prefer penalization on tiles with \emph{low to moderate} flow coverage. This is the regime where alias/motion dominates and where down--scaling scores is most beneficial. Dense flow tiles are either left to STAP alone or are protected via the flow coherence check. (In the current implementation, coverage is treated as an \emph{upper} bound; missing coverage defaults to ``pass'' in this test.)

\subsection{G--3: Baseline score quantile window}

Let $S_{\text{base}}(z)$ be the baseline BR/GLRT score. Within a given depth stratum, compute score quantiles $q_{0.3},q_{0.95}$ over BG tiles. Gate requires
\[
S_{\text{base}}(z)\in [q_{0.3},q_{0.95}].
\]

Interpretation: avoid penalization on the noise floor (very low scores) and on already high--confidence positives (very high scores). This focuses KA on the part of the negative tail that matters near the target FPR.

\subsection{G--4: Registration quality (optional)}

If motion compensation / registration is used, include a registration quality metric (e.g.\ PSR) and require
\[
\mathrm{PSR}_{\text{reg}}\le \text{PSR}_{\max},
\]
or any similar condition ensuring that tiles with gross registration failure are handled separately.

\subsection{Offline validation of gating}

On a held--out labeled configuration, define
\[
p_+ := \mathbb{P}\{g=1\mid H_1\},\qquad
p_- := \mathbb{P}\{g=1\mid H_0\},
\]
for the combined gate $g$ that incorporates G--1, G--2, G--2', G--3, G--4. Require:
\[
p_-\ge 0.2,\quad p_- \le 0.5,\quad p_+ \le 0.1 p_-.
\]

Interpretation: a nontrivial fraction of negatives are gated (but not too many), while positives are rarely gated. This condition is central to the SL--2 quantile shift argument.

\subsection{Summary: Compact Feasibility Checklist (Updated)}

KA--STAP can be expected to outperform MC--SVD (or STAP--only) in the low-FPR regime if and only if:

\begin{enumerate}[label=\arabic*.,nosep]
  \item \textbf{Regime preconditions.} Band occupancy R--1, alias separation R--2, and baseline headroom R--3 are satisfied on a held--out validation set.
  \item \textbf{Band design.} BG--1/BG--2/BG--3 hold for the chosen PRF/$T$/DPSS; Pf/Pa are physically meaningful (e.g.\ Pf $\approx$ 30--250 Hz, Pa $\gtrsim$ 400 Hz in brain fUS).
  \item \textbf{Operator geometry.} OC--1 with $\varepsilon_{\text{mix}}\le 0.05$ and OC--2 with $\Lambda = s_a/s_f \ge 1.25$ and $\delta_\bullet\le 0.1$.
  \item \textbf{Blending realizable.} CB--1/CB--2/CB--3 satisfied with chosen $(\beta,\delta)$.
  \item \textbf{Gating conservative and class--differential.} G--1/G--2/G--2'/G--3/G--4 implemented; offline, $p_-\in[0.2,0.5]$ and $p_+\le 0.1p_-$.
  \item \textbf{Regime sentinel.} RS--1 does not trigger (KA not out of regime).
  \item \textbf{Invariance sentinel.} $\wh s_a/\wh s_f \notin[1-\epsilon,1+\epsilon]$ with $\epsilon\le 0.02$; otherwise KA is effectively scalar and can be skipped.
  \item \textbf{Safety guard.} Safety--1 (monotonicity on gated flow tiles) passes.
  \item \textbf{Evaluation discipline.} GLRT controls and tail CI practices (see \ifSTAPSUPPLEMENTONLY the main paper\else Section~\ref{sec:eval}\fi) in place for fair low--FPR comparison.
\end{enumerate}

When all of the above hold, KA applies a non--mixing, alias--inflating band transform chiefly to alias--dominant tiles, pushing negative scores down while largely leaving positives unchanged. This lowers the negative $\alpha$--quantile and increases TPR at fixed small FPR in a manner consistent with the SL--2 quantile shift analysis.
