\section*{Appendix A: Draft Claims}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Appendix A: Draft Claims}

\begin{enumerate}
\item A computer-implemented method for generating an ultrasound score map, comprising:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
\item receiving beamformed ultrasound data or residualized slow-time ultrasound data;
\item partitioning the data into a plurality of localized spatial support regions;
\item forming, for each localized spatial support region, one or more slow-time vectors or slow-time snapshots;
\item computing, from the one or more slow-time vectors or slow-time snapshots, a matched-subspace detector score using a target flow-consistent subspace and at least one non-target component; and
\item aggregating localized detector scores to generate an output score map, detection map, or vascular map.
\end{enumerate}

\item The method of claim 1, wherein computing the matched-subspace detector score comprises computing a non-whitened or identity-whitened band-limited matched-subspace score.

\item The method of claim 1, wherein the target flow-consistent subspace is defined at least in part by a flow band, and wherein one or more additional subspaces are defined by a guard band, an alias band, or an out-of-band complement.

\item The method of claim 1, further comprising:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
\item computing a clutter-evidence feature for at least one localized spatial support region; and
\item selecting, based on the clutter-evidence feature, between a fixed detector branch and a fully whitened detector branch.
\end{enumerate}

\item The method of claim 4, wherein the clutter-evidence feature is derived from guard-band energy, guard-band fraction, alias-band energy, alias-to-flow ratio, motion information, a covariance-condition metric, or a combination thereof.

\item The method of claim 4, wherein the fully whitened detector branch comprises estimating a localized covariance matrix, conditioning the localized covariance matrix, whitening localized data using the conditioned covariance matrix, and computing a covariance-adaptive detector score from whitened localized data.

\item The method of claim 1, further comprising applying a shrink-only penalty to at least a subset of localized detector scores, wherein the shrink-only penalty is constrained not to increase any detector score.

\item The method of claim 7, wherein applying the shrink-only penalty comprises:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
\item identifying a candidate set eligible for suppression;
\item identifying a protected set excluded from suppression; and
\item generating a monotone non-increasing weight or suppression transform from side-information features.
\end{enumerate}

\item The method of claim 1, further comprising determining a threshold from calibration data and applying the threshold to held-out deployment data without per-window recalibration.

\item The method of claim 1, wherein the localized detector scores are produced using an exact-output-preserving execution architecture comprising batched tile extraction, reusable localized operators, conditional branch execution, and overlap-add aggregation.

\item The method of claim 10, wherein the reusable localized operators comprise one or more cached overlap counts, geometry terms, projector terms, or support operators that are reused across multiple windows or batches.

\item A system comprising:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
\item an ultrasound data interface configured to receive beamformed ultrasound data or residualized slow-time ultrasound data;
\item one or more processors; and
\item memory storing instructions that, when executed by the one or more processors, cause the one or more processors to perform the method of claim 1.
\end{enumerate}

\item A non-transitory computer-readable medium storing instructions that, when executed by one or more processors, cause the one or more processors to perform the method of claim 1.
\end{enumerate}
